World Health Organization (WHO) and Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) guidelines for managing appointment adherence and proactive care coordination:

1. Send automated SMS or voice call reminders at least 48 hours before the scheduled appointment for all high-risk patients.
2. Offer flexible rescheduling options, including telehealth visits, for patients with long lead times (greater than 15 days) between booking and the appointment dates.
3. Prioritize personal outreach and follow-up phone calls for patients on algorithms who did not receive an SMS confirmation.
4. Consider implementing same-day confirmation calls for patients with a documented prior history of no-shows.
5. Engage social workers or patient navigators for patients demonstrating multiple chronic conditions such as Hypertension and Diabetes, as they face higher barriers to access.
6. Assess potential transportation and financial barriers when developing individual care plans.
7. Conduct regular audits of appointment wait times and strive to reduce lead times for vulnerable demographics.
8. Patient privacy must be strictly maintained; all interventions must fully comply with local medical and healthcare regulations.
9. Care coordination interventions are advisory and must supplement, not replace, clinical judgment by qualified healthcare professionals.
10. Systematically document the reasons for missed appointments to continuously improve scheduling policies and outreach programs.
